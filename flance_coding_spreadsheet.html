Data Coding Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Your Data

1. Copy all family responses from your survey into the "Response Coding" tab
2. Include response ID, question type, and full family response text
3. One row per response segment (may need multiple rows for long responses)

Step 2: Apply Primary Codes

FH-LG (Family Hopes & Learning Goals): Use when families discuss aspirations, goals, school readiness, child development hopes
UN-C (Unmet Needs & Concerns): Use for barriers, challenges, gaps in service, stressors, problems
TS-WC (Trusted Staff & Welcoming Culture): Use for positive staff interactions, feeling welcome, safety, trust
ST-TC (Strengthen Trust & Communication): Use for suggestions, improvement ideas, communication requests
BN-WS (Basic Needs & Wraparound Support): Use for housing, food, transportation, financial, material needs
HN-DLS (Health, Nutrition & Daily Life Skills): Use for healthy habits, nutrition, hygiene, social-emotional skills

Step 3: Apply Secondary Codes (if applicable)

Some responses may fit multiple categories. Use secondary coding when a response clearly addresses two themes. For example: "I love how the teachers help my child eat healthy foods, but I wish they would tell me more about what he eats each day" would be:
Primary: HN-DLS (health/nutrition focus)
Secondary: ST-TC (communication suggestion)

Step 4: Assign Sentiment

Positive (+): Appreciation, satisfaction, praise, gratitude
Neutral (0): Factual statements, neutral suggestions, balanced feedback
Negative (-): Concerns, complaints, frustration, disappointment

Step 5: Determine Priority Level

High: Urgent needs, serious concerns, safety issues, repeated themes
Medium: Important improvements, helpful suggestions, moderate concerns
Low: Nice-to-have improvements, minor issues, general positive feedback

Step 6: Analyze and Report

1. Review the Analysis Summary tab for patterns
2. Identify the most frequent codes and high-priority items
3. Look for intersections (e.g., communication issues + trust concerns)
4. Develop action plans based on coded themes
5. Share findings with team using evidence from coded responses

Quality Assurance Tips

Inter-rater Reliability: Have a second person code 10-20% of responses to check consistency
Code Definitions: Refer back to the codebook regularly to maintain consistency
Context Matters: Consider the full response context, not just keywords
Family Voice: When in doubt, ask "What is this family really trying to tell us?"
